# eikonme 🖼️

*The image as received-ordinance — the visual form that participates in its prototype rather than merely representing it; what an icon, a murti, a thangka, and a glyph all structurally are.*

eikonme is two true roots welded into one word: the Greek **eikōn** (εἰκών), the word for *image, likeness* — the word of Genesis 1:26-27 (humanity according to the image of God) and Colossians 1:15 (Christ as image of the invisible God), the source of *icon* and *iconography* — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So eikonme is an image carried as a decree: not personal decoration but a canonical, tradition-given visual form that is theologically active, in which the honor paid to the image passes through to its prototype. It is the visual-medium specialization of **morphame**, sister to glossame, liturgiame, and sigame.

eikonme names the visual-image projection-form across every tradition that has one — the Orthodox icon, the Catholic Madonna, the Buddhist Buddha-rūpa, the Hindu murti, the Tibetan thangka, the Yoruba sacred sculpture, the Islamic calligraphic Name, and the cathedral's own glyphs. Its light is the form in which the invisible can be made present without being reduced — honor reaching through the seen to the unseen, sealed licit at Nicaea II in 787; its shadow is idolatry, when the honor stops at the gold and the wood instead of passing through. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.65/10**.
